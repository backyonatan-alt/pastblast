How to Play

Everything you need to know to get started

What is PastBlast?

PastBlast is a multiplayer party game about history, landmarks, and world flags. One person hosts the game on a big screen, and everyone else plays on their phones. Think Kahoot, but for geography and history nerds!

Setting Up a Game

  1. 💻 The host opens PastBlast on a laptop or TV and taps "Host a New Game"
  2. 📱 Players open PastBlast on their phones and enter the 4-letter room code shown on the host screen (or scan the QR code)
  3. ✏️ Each player enters their name and taps "Join Game"
  4. ⚙️ The host picks the game mode, difficulty, and game length
  5. 🚀 The host taps "Start Game" and you're off!

Game Modes

🏛️

Timeline

A card appears with a historical event or famous landmark. You need to place it in the correct position on your timeline — is it earlier or later than the cards already there?

  • Each player builds their own timeline
  • Correct placement = the card stays on your timeline (+1 point)
  • Wrong placement = the card is discarded
  • The player with the most cards on their timeline wins!
Tip: Look at the "Earlier" and "Later" labels on your phone to understand the direction. Tap a "Place here" button — don't try to drag!
🏁

Flag Quiz

A flag appears on screen. You need to identify the country by typing its name. Start typing and pick from the autocomplete suggestions.

  • Correct answer = +1 point
  • You have 30 seconds per flag
  • The player with the most correct answers wins!
Tip: You don't need to spell the full name — just start typing and the suggestions will appear. For example, type "uni" to find United States or United Kingdom.

Stealing

When a player gets an answer wrong, other players get a chance to steal!

  1. The wrong answer is not revealed yet
  2. Each other player gets a turn to answer (with a 15-second timer)
  3. If you steal correctly: +1 point for you!
  4. If you steal wrong: -1 point penalty
  5. You can always tap "Pass" to skip without risk
  6. The answer is revealed after everyone has had their chance
Tip: Stealing is risky! Only go for it if you're confident. Passing is always safe.

Playing Without Phones

Don't have phones? No problem! Click "Play Locally" on the host screen to play on a single device. Pick the number of players (1-6), and take turns on the same screen.

Pro Tips

  • Project it! The host screen is designed for big screens and projectors — perfect for classrooms or parties
  • QR code makes joining fast — no need to type the URL
  • Mix it up — play a round of Timeline then switch to Flag Quiz
  • Use stealing strategically — if you know the answer, steal it! If not, pass and save your points
  • Hard mode is really hard — try Easy first if you're new to history
